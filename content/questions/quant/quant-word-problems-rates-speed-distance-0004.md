## Question

A car travels from town A to town B at an average speed of 60 mph, and then returns along the
same road at an average speed of 40 mph. What is the car's average speed for the entire round
trip?

## Explanation

Average speed is **total distance ÷ total time** — never the simple average of the two speeds.

Because the two legs cover the *same distance*, pick a convenient distance. Let each leg be 120
miles (a common multiple of 60 and 40):

- Going: \(120 \div 60 = 2\) hours
- Returning: \(120 \div 40 = 3\) hours

Total distance \(= 240\) miles; total time \(= 5\) hours.

\[\text{average speed} = \frac{240}{5} = 48 \text{ mph}\]

**The trap:** answer **C (50 mph)** is the plain average of 60 and 40. That's wrong because the
car spends *more time* at the slower speed (3 hours at 40 vs. 2 hours at 60), which pulls the
average below 50.

## Hints

- Average speed = total distance / total time. Set the two speeds' simple average aside.
- Choose a distance that both speeds divide evenly (e.g. 120), compute the time for each leg, then combine.
