## Question

If \(x^2 - 5x + 6 = 0\), what is the sum of all possible values of \(x\)?

## Explanation

Factor the quadratic. You need two numbers that multiply to \(+6\) and add to \(-5\): those are
\(-2\) and \(-3\).

\[x^2 - 5x + 6 = (x - 2)(x - 3) = 0\]

So \(x = 2\) or \(x = 3\), and their sum is \(2 + 3 = 5\).

**Shortcut:** for \(x^2 + bx + c = 0\), the sum of the roots is \(-b\) (here \(-(-5) = 5\)) and the
product is \(c\) (here 6). You can read the answer straight off the coefficients without solving.

**The trap:** answer **C (6)** is the *product* of the roots, not the sum. Choice **A (1)** is the
*difference*.

## Hints

- Factor into \((x - r_1)(x - r_2)\); the roots make each factor zero.
- Or use the coefficient shortcut: sum of roots \(= -b\), product \(= c\).
