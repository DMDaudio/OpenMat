## Question

If \(\tfrac{1}{3}\) of a number is 12, what is \(\tfrac{3}{4}\) of that number?

## Explanation

Work in two steps: first recover the whole number, then take the fraction you actually want.

If \(\tfrac{1}{3}\) of the number is 12, the whole number is \(12 \times 3 = 36\).

Then \(\tfrac{3}{4}\) of 36 is:

\[\frac{3}{4} \times 36 = 27\]

So the answer is **27**.

**The trap:** answer **D (36)** is the *whole* number — easy to stop there if you forget the
question asked for three-quarters of it. Choice **A (16)** comes from mistakenly taking
\(\tfrac{4}{3}\) or similar slips.

## Hints

- First find the whole number from the "\(\tfrac{1}{3}\) is 12" fact.
- Then multiply by \(\tfrac{3}{4}\) — and re-read to confirm which quantity is being asked for.
