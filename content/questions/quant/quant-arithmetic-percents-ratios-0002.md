## Question

The price of a jacket is first increased by 25%, and then the new, higher price is decreased
by 20%. The final price is what percent of the original price?

## Explanation

Successive percent changes **multiply** — you cannot add or subtract the percentages.

Let the original price be \(P\). After a 25% increase the price is \(1.25P\). Decreasing that by
20% multiplies it by \(0.80\):

\[1.25P \times 0.80 = 1.00P\]

So the final price is exactly **100%** of the original — the two changes cancel.

**The trap:** answer **D (105%)** comes from wrongly *adding* the changes (\(+25\% - 20\% = +5\%\)).
That ignores that the 20% decrease applies to the *larger* post-increase price, not the original.

## Hints

- Percent changes compound multiplicatively. Turn each into a factor: +25% → \(\times 1.25\), −20% → \(\times 0.80\).
- Multiply the factors together and compare to 1.
