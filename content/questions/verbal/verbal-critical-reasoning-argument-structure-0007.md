## Question

The new tax on sugary drinks will not reduce obesity. People who want sugary drinks will simply
buy them in neighboring counties that impose no such tax. Indeed, a similar tax in another region
led to a sharp rise in cross-border shopping with no measurable drop in obesity.

Which of the following is the main conclusion of the argument?

## Explanation

The main conclusion is the claim the rest of the passage exists to support. Test each sentence
with "*because*": the conclusion is what the other statements give reasons *for*.

- "The tax will not reduce obesity" — *why?* Because people will buy elsewhere, and a similar tax
  did the same. The other sentences support this one. This is the **conclusion**. **(A) is correct.**
- "People will buy in neighboring counties" **(B)** and "a similar tax raised cross-border
  shopping" **(C)** are the **premises** — the support offered.
- **(D)** is an unstated background assumption, not the conclusion.
- **(E)** is broader than anything the argument claims; the argument is about *this* tax, not all taxation.

## Hints

- The conclusion is the point being argued *for*; premises are the reasons given.
- Insert "therefore" before each candidate — the sentence the others lead into is the conclusion.
