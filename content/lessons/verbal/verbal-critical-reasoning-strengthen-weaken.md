## Overview

Strengthen and Weaken questions are the most common Critical Reasoning type. Both turn on the
same skill: finding the argument's **assumption** — the unstated gap between its evidence and its
conclusion — and then either protecting that gap (strengthen) or exposing it (weaken).

## Core concepts

**Map the argument first.** Separate the **conclusion** (the claim being pushed) from the
**premises** (the support). Then ask: *what has to be true for the premises to lead to this
conclusion?* That's the assumption.

- **Weaken** = attack the assumption. The correct answer makes the conclusion *less likely* given the same evidence.
- **Strengthen** = defend the assumption. The correct answer makes the conclusion *more likely*.

**Causal arguments are the favorite target.** When an argument concludes "X caused Y" from the
fact that X and Y occurred together, the assumption is "nothing else explains Y." You can:

- **Weaken** by offering an *alternative cause* for Y, or showing Y happened without X.
- **Strengthen** by *ruling out* alternative causes, or showing that removing X removes Y.

## Worked examples

**Weaken.** *A store added self-checkout lanes; the next month, checkout complaints dropped.
Therefore self-checkout reduced complaints.* This assumes nothing else changed. An answer noting
that the store also **doubled its staff that month** offers an alternative cause and weakens it.

**Strengthen.** For the same argument, an answer stating that **staffing and product prices were
unchanged that month** rules out alternatives and strengthens the causal claim.

## Common traps

- **Out-of-scope answers.** Choices about a related-but-irrelevant issue (cost, popularity,
  fairness) feel weighty but don't touch the conclusion. Stay ruthlessly on the exact conclusion.
- **Wrong direction.** Under time pressure it's easy to pick a strengthener on a weaken question.
  Re-read the stem before committing.
- **"Too strong" strengtheners.** You don't need an answer that *proves* the conclusion — just one
  that makes it more likely. Beware distractors that overreach or introduce new assumptions.

## Key takeaways

- Find the conclusion, the premises, and the assumption bridging them.
- Weaken = break the assumption (often via an alternative cause); Strengthen = protect it (often by ruling alternatives out).
- Discard out-of-scope answers and always confirm which direction the stem asks for.
